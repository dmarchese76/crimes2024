Reported Rapes in NYC Up Nearly 20% in 2024

Manhattan neighborhoods like Times Square and Chelsea experience sharp increases as reporting rates rise.

Delfina Marchese

Nearly 20 percent more rapes have been reported in New York City to date in 2024 compared to 2023, according to NYPD data published and updated weekly.

In Manhattan, the Times Square area saw the most significant increase, with reported cases doubling to 29 this year compared to 14 in 2023. A similar trend was observed in the Chelsea and Hudson Yards neighborhoods, which reported 14 cases this year, up from five in 2023.

A press release from the NYPD released on December 3rd said: “Year to date in 2024, major crime across New York City is reduced 1.9%,” Including significant decreases in murders, robberies and burglary. However, it also acknowledges a rise in rape, with 46 additional cases reported in November, nearly half of which originated from incidents in prior years and weren't reported until now.

Police commissioner Jessica S. Tisch said in the announcement that she attributes the crime reduction to strategic deployment and increased patrol efforts.

Associate professor of John Jay College of Criminal Justice said that he can't say what contributes to the growth in rape cases in NYC. “ It could be something simple, increases in the reporting rate,” he said . “ or it could be much higher counts or incidents.”
